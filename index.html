Our Sponsors:

The American Communities Project is a combined social science/journalism effort based at the Michigan State University of Journalism. Their purpose is to use vast arrays of data to break communities into different types for analysis. - Working with academics, the ACP has used a wide range of different factors - everything from income to race and ethnicity to education to religious affiliation - as well as a clustering technique to identify 15 types of communities, everything from Big Cities to Aging Farmlands.

We also wanted to give an honorable mention to USA Facts as they were the ones able to provide us with the data needed to continue this project.

Problem Statement:

How can ACP identify the relationship between different communities and temperature/precipitation in order to tell a story of how these communities are impacting the climate?
